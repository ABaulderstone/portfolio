About me

I’m a Junior Developer based in Sydney. Seeking a change in career, I moved from Adelaide, to Sydney in early 2019. This has also allowed me an opportunity to focus on personal development and start fresh.

I grew up in a very remote area in rural South Australia, during my time there I discovered the immense communicative potential of the internet. Between that and my first forays into programming with BASIC I developed a passion for technology.

As a Developer, I want to merge two of my biggest passions; technology and social justice. I want to use the skills I’ve honed in order to amplify marginalised voices, improve accessibility, and generally make the world a more inclusive place.

Education

After moving to Adelaide in 2007 I have studied in numerous fields. I began a Bachelor of Economics at the University of Adelaide in 2008, although I received excellent marks in all core subjects it didn’t hold my interest, so I transferred into a Bachelor of Arts - majoring in English and Linguistics - in 2009. My main areas of interest were Linguistic Anthropology, the emerging field of Internet Linguistics, and Australian Literature. In 2010 I was awarded the Brian Elliot Prize for Australian Literary studies.

I am currently studying a Diploma of IT delivered in a bootcamp format through Coder Academy. The course Covers Ruby on Rails, HTML/CSS, Javascript and the MERN stack.
